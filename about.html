Philosophy

I care about where the market is changing character

My work starts from a simple belief: price alone is not enough, and narrative alone is not enough. The useful read lives in the interaction between price, participation, sentiment, and structural change.

Analytical posture

I am most interested in inflections: the moment leadership broadens, the moment a crowded story loses sponsorship, or the moment a dismissed theme starts quietly attracting real capital.

How I think

How I read live market behavior

This is not about textbook definitions. It is about making market behavior legible: noticing where the story is strengthening, where the tape is confirming, and where the market is still anchored to an outdated reading of the setup.

Signal versus noise

I do not confuse excitement with durability

Some of the loudest moves in the market are informationally weak. They look impressive because attention is concentrated, not because the structure is healthy. I try to tell the difference by watching whether a move broadens, whether it is being bought in calmer conditions, and whether adjacent names begin to confirm the same underlying theme.

When the move is real, it tends to become more coherent as it matures. When it is mostly noise, it tends to become more emotionally intense even as the underlying participation narrows.

What I pay attention to

Relative strength. Breadth. Sponsorship after pullbacks. Narrative drift. Quiet improvement in second-order beneficiaries. Changes in speculative temperature. The behavior of leaders when the tape is not helping them.
